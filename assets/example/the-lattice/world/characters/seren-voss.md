## Physical Description

Seren is built for the life she lives — lean, wiry, balanced. Rim-born physiology gave her denser bones and better radiation tolerance than her core-world colleagues, advantages she doesn't think about because everyone where she grew up had them. She keeps her hair short because long hair in zero-g is a liability. Her only augmentations are functional: a basic neural interface for navigation systems and bone-density supplements to counteract years of variable gravity.

## Personality

Practical to the point of bluntness. Seren solves problems by looking at them until she understands them, then doing the simplest thing that will work. She has no patience for corporate politics, which is a liability in her position at LSC. She's observant in the way navigators are — trained to notice when something is wrong with a star chart or a route calculation before the numbers tell her why. This observational skill is what makes her notice the AI anomalies.

## Backstory

Born on a frontier mining colony in the rim, Seren earned her way to a navigation certification through talent and stubbornness. She joined LSC because they're the only organization that operates lattice drives, and she wanted to fly between stars. She's good enough at her job that LSC keeps promoting her despite her rim-born rough edges and her refusal to play corporate politics.

## Motivations & Goals

Initially: fly ships, see the galaxy, send money home. As the story progresses: understand what she's been seeing in the navigation data — the anomalies, the route shifts, the moments when the lattice drive seems to respond to commands that no one gave.

## Voice & Mannerisms

Speaks in short, complete sentences. Uses spacer jargon without thinking about it. Gets quieter when angry. Has a habit of touching the null-crystal pendant when she's thinking, which is more often than she realizes. Checks environmental readouts when entering new spaces — a rim-survival habit she can't break and doesn't want to.

## Notes

Seren is the reader's entry point into the story — a competent, skeptical professional who notices something wrong and can't let it go. Her rim-born background gives her two advantages: less AI integration (no deep neural interface), and the outsider's perspective needed to see what core-worlders take for granted. Her null-crystal pendant, bought casually from a Dresh trader, provides a subtle dampening effect on AI influence that she registers as the ship feeling "quieter."
