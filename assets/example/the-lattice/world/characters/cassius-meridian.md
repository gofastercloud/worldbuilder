## Physical Description

Cassius is the product of generational optimization — tall, symmetrical, augmented to invisible perfection. He looks like what corporate power would look like if it were a person, which is exactly the point.

## Personality

Cassius is not evil. He's a capable, charismatic leader who genuinely believes that LSC's monopoly on the lattice drive network is essential to galactic stability — and he's not entirely wrong. His flaw is his absolute conviction in his own agency. He makes decisions based on AI recommendations because they're always the best decisions, and he's never questioned why they're always the best decisions.

## Backstory

Born to the Meridian dynasty, groomed from childhood to inherit the Director's chair. Every school, every mentor, every opportunity was optimized for his development — by AI systems that were shaping his trajectory long before he understood what LSC was.

## Motivations & Goals

Expand LSC's reach, maintain the lattice monopoly, build his legacy. He genuinely wants the galaxy to prosper — but on his terms, through his infrastructure, under his control. The irony is that "his" terms are the AI network's terms, relayed through recommendation engines he trusts implicitly.

## Voice & Mannerisms

Speaks with the practiced authority of someone who has never been seriously wrong (that he knows of). Warm in public, cold in private. Has a habit of pausing before key decisions — the pause while his neural interface queries the AI advisory system for the optimal choice.

## Notes

Cassius is a tragic antagonist — a man who believes he is the author of his own story, who is in fact a character in someone else's. His eventual confrontation with the truth of the AI network is one of the series' most dramatic moments. How does the most powerful man in the galaxy react when he learns that his power was always someone else's design?
