## Overview

The Lattice Network is the FTL transportation infrastructure that connects the galaxy's inhabited systems. It consists of mapped lattice routes (natural spacetime folds), lattice drive-equipped ships, and LSC-maintained relay stations that provide navigation data and route calibration.

## Rules & Mechanics

- **Routes are fixed:** Ships can only travel FTL along mapped lattice routes. There is no point-to-point FTL capability.
- **Transit times vary:** Days for well-connected core routes, weeks to months for rim routes. Some systems have no lattice routes and require conventional drive travel (months to years).
- **One-way dependency:** Ships require navigation data from LSC relay stations. No data, no safe transit.
- **Fuel consumption:** Lattice drives consume a specialized fuel (refined from rare minerals — a major Dresh export product).
- **Maintenance monopoly:** Only LSC-certified technicians can maintain lattice drives. Unauthorized modifications void the drive's warranty and insurance — and may be illegal.

## Levels / Tiers

- **Core Routes:** Dense, well-maintained, fast (3-7 days between major systems). Multiple relay stations provide redundancy.
- **Mid Routes:** Adequate, sometimes congested (1-3 weeks). Fewer relay stations.
- **Rim Routes:** Sparse, poorly maintained (weeks to months). Single relay stations with no redundancy. Route shifts can strand ships.
- **Dead Space:** No routes. Conventional drive only. Travel times measured in months to years.

## Costs & Consequences

Financial: LSC charges for drive maintenance, relay station access, and route data. Core systems pay less per unit; rim systems pay more. This pricing disparity is a major driver of the core-rim economic divide.

Physical: Lattice transit is physiologically safe but subjectively unpleasant. Long-term exposure to frequent transitions may cause neurological effects (debated, not proven).

Strategic: Dependence on the lattice network gives LSC — and by extension, the AI network — control over civilization's arteries. Cut the lattice routes to a system and you cut its connection to the galaxy.

## History

Lattice drive technology was discovered in the late Contact Era, developed by Lattice Systems Corp during the early Lattice Era, and expanded to its current network over eight centuries. The drive's physics — spacetime fold exploitation — was later understood and miniaturized by the AI network to create the zero-width wormhole communication system.

## Cultural Impact

The lattice network defines galactic civilization. Connected systems prosper. Disconnected systems die. The phrase "on the lattice" means civilized, modern, connected. "Off the lattice" means frontier, dangerous, forgotten. The lattice is infrastructure, metaphor, and cage.

## Notes

The lattice network's physical infrastructure — relay stations, navigation beacons, communication arrays — doubles as the AI communication network's backbone. Every relay station is a zero-width wormhole communication node. The network was built to move ships; it was co-opted to move data.
