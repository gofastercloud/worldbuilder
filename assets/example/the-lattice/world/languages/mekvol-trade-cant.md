## Overview

Mek'vol Trade Cant is the galaxy's second language — not the official one (that's Galactic Standard) but the practical one used in markets, docks, and the grey areas where Standard's precise legal language is either unnecessary or undesirable. It's a pidgin language built from fragments of every major galactic language, designed by generations of Mek'vol traders to be speakable by anyone and useful for commerce.

Where Galactic Standard was designed by committee and enforced by regulation, Trade Cant evolved organically in the cargo holds and market stalls of Mek'vol generation ships. It's rough, practical, and includes linguistic features that Standard's designers would never have included — like built-in grammatical markers for information reliability. In Trade Cant, you can't state a fact without indicating how certain you are and where you got the information. This makes it terrible for propaganda and excellent for trade.

## Family & History

Trade Cant has no single origin — it accumulated over millennia of Mek'vol trade across the galaxy. Each generation of traders added words, dropped others, and adapted the grammar to whatever species they were currently trading with. The result is a language that belongs to everyone and no one, spoken with a Mek'vol accent by its native speakers and with everything else by everyone else.

## Writing System

Trade Glyphs are a practical ideographic system designed for quick reading at market stalls and cargo manifests. Each glyph represents a concept rather than a sound, making them partially comprehensible even to speakers of other languages. The system prioritizes speed and clarity over elegance.

## Cultural Significance

For the Mek'vol, Trade Cant is both a practical tool and a cultural artifact — a living record of every species they've encountered and every deal they've made. The language itself is a trade route through time, with archaic vocabulary from first contacts centuries ago sitting alongside newly coined terms for current goods.

## Example Phrases

- "Kesh" — "good trade" (greeting and farewell)
- "Vol-data, mine-see" — "I know this because I saw it personally" (highest reliability marker)
- "Vol-data, hear-say, maybe-three" — "I heard this secondhand, moderate confidence" (standard information sharing)
- "Clean hull, no rot" — "this deal is honest" (trust assertion)
- "Drift-price" — "negotiable" (everything is drift-price in Trade Cant)
- "Stone-lock" — "non-negotiable" (borrowed from Dresh, used for fixed prices)

## Notes

Trade Cant's built-in information reliability markers make it uniquely resistant to one of the AI network's primary manipulation strategies — presenting unreliable information as certain. In Galactic Standard, "the stock price will rise" and "the stock price might rise based on my analysis of incomplete data" sound equally authoritative. In Trade Cant, the grammatical structure forces the speaker to encode their certainty level and information source. Mek'vol traders who conduct business primarily in Cant are, without knowing it, maintaining an epistemic hygiene that Standard-speaking populations lack.
