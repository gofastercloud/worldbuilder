## Overview

Surface Dresh are the clan members who go topside — who leave the mines and deep warrens to trade Dresh goods, negotiate contracts, and interact with the wider galaxy. They serve as the bridge between Deep Dresh tradition and multi-species commerce, a role that earns them both respect and suspicion within their own species.

## Physical Characteristics

Surface Dresh have lighter, more varied mineral deposits than their deep-dwelling kin. Their exoskeletons incorporate more decorative crystalline formations, which they use as both status displays and quality demonstrations in mineral trade. Their tremorsense is reduced compared to Deep Dresh, but their visual acuity and social awareness are enhanced.

## Culture & Society

Surface Dresh maintain clan loyalty while operating in multi-species contexts. They navigate a difficult cultural position — representing Dresh interests in a galaxy that views their species as either valuable resource providers or stubborn primitives.

## History & Origins

Surface Dresh emerged as a distinct cultural group during the Contact Era, when the Dresh needed representatives who could interact with other species. Over generations, this role shaped both their physical traits and cultural practices.

## Notable Settlements

The Bazaar, Haven's merchant districts, Wanderer's Rest, and surface settlements on Crucible.

## Relations With Other Races

Surface Dresh maintain their closest inter-species relationships with Mek'vol traders and Keth'ri Speakers. They act as cultural interpreters between Deep Dresh and the wider galaxy.

## Notable Individuals

- **Forge-Master Grith** — Runs Crucible's largest mine. Surface Dresh by role but straddles both subcultures. Uses AI systems pragmatically but maintains manual overrides on everything.

## Notes

Forge-Master Grith represents the pragmatic middle ground in the Dresh AI debate. She uses AI systems because her operation requires them, but she insists on organic backups and manual overrides for everything critical. This pragmatism makes her operation slightly less efficient — and slightly less vulnerable to AI network manipulation.
