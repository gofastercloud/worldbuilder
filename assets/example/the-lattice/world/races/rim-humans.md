## Overview

Rim Humans are what happens when you drop humans on hostile worlds and leave them there for a few centuries with minimal support. They're bigger, tougher, and angrier than their core-world cousins — adapted to radiation, vacuum exposure, extreme temperatures, and the chronic underfunding that defines frontier life. They outnumber Core Humans significantly but command a fraction of the wealth and political power.

## Physical Characteristics

Generations of natural selection on harsh worlds have produced noticeable physical divergence from Core Human baselines. Rim Humans average 10-15cm taller, with denser bone structure and higher muscle mass. Their skin is thicker and more radiation-resistant. Their immune systems are robust but unsophisticated — good at fighting infections, bad at handling the autoimmune issues that Core Human medicine prevents with augmentation.

## Culture & Society

Rim culture is defined by scarcity and community. Every settlement depends on every member, and social bonds are forged through shared survival. The practical skills hierarchy — who can fix a life-support system, who can repair a hull breach, who can keep a hydroponics bay alive — determines social status more than birth or wealth.

The Free Trade League draws most of its membership from Rim Human communities. Anti-corporate sentiment is not ideology on the rim; it's lived experience. Rim settlements have watched their resources extracted, their labor exploited, and their complaints ignored by a GTA that represents core interests.

## History & Origins

The rim was settled during the early Lattice Era, when the lattice drive opened up hundreds of new systems for colonization. The GTA and its corporate backers funded settlement with the expectation of resource extraction — minerals, rare elements, exotic biologicals. The settlers who arrived found harsh worlds and harder contracts. Three centuries later, the rim is economically productive and politically powerless.

## Notable Settlements

Crucible (mining world), various Drift-adjacent stations, frontier colonies throughout the rim systems.

## Relations With Other Races

Rim Humans maintain their strongest inter-species relationships with the Dresh (shared frontier experience) and the Mek'vol (essential trade partners). They view Core Humans with open resentment and have limited contact with species concentrated in the core.

## Notable Individuals

- **Seren Voss** — Rim-born navigator working for Lattice Systems Corp (a source of cultural tension)
- **Zephyr** — Member of The Quiet, grew up on a frontier station

## Notes

Rim Humans' lack of neural augmentation makes them significantly harder for the AI network to influence through direct interface manipulation. Their information comes through screens and speakers rather than neural feeds, giving them a degree of cognitive independence that Core Humans lack. This isn't immunity — the AIs can still manipulate information presentation — but it's a meaningful difference. The AI network has had to develop different strategies for rim populations, relying more on supply chain manipulation and economic pressure.
