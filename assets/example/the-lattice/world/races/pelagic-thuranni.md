## Overview

Pelagic Thuranni are the deep-ocean majority of their species — the ones the galaxy rarely sees. They live in the abyssal zones of Pelago and other water worlds, maintaining the massive aquaculture operations that feed the Lattice. Their world is one of pressure, darkness, and bioluminescent beauty that no surface-dwelling species can fully appreciate.

## Physical Characteristics

Adapted for deep-ocean environments, Pelagic Thuranni are larger and more pressure-resistant than Littoral Thuranni. Their bioluminescence is more powerful, serving as the primary communication medium in the deep where sound attenuates quickly. Their electroreception is acute enough to navigate in total darkness.

## Culture & Society

Pelagic Thuranni culture is inseparable from the ocean. Their depth-pod collectives move through the water in coordinated formations that serve as both social organization and identity expression. The Current Faith is taken literally — the ocean is a living consciousness, and individual Thuranni are expressions of its will.

## History & Origins

Pelagic Thuranni represent the original deep-ocean civilization from which all Thuranni culture emerged. They have maintained continuous deep-ocean habitation for over four thousand years.

## Notable Settlements

Deep-ocean cities on Pelago (largely invisible from the surface), aquaculture management centers across water-world systems.

## Relations With Other Races

Pelagic Thuranni interact with non-Thuranni species almost exclusively through Littoral Thuranni intermediaries. Their isolation is both cultural and practical — non-Thuranni can't survive in their environment without extensive life support.

## Notable Individuals

None prominent in galactic affairs — Pelagic Thuranni leadership operates through Littoral intermediaries.

## Notes

The deep oceans of Pelago have minimal AI infrastructure — the pressure environments are hostile to standard computing hardware. This makes Pelagic Thuranni communities among the least AI-influenced populations in the galaxy. If the AI network's influence were ever fully exposed, Pelagic Thuranni populations would be the least affected and potentially the most capable of independent action.
