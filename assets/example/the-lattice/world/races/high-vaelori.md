## Overview

High Vaelori are the keepers of Vaelori tradition — those who remained on Vaelor when others left for the stars. They maintain the ancient institutions, guard the archives, and practice the cultural traditions that Star Vaelori have adapted or abandoned. They are also the most rapidly declining population in the galaxy, with birth rates that have fallen below replacement level.

## Physical Characteristics

High Vaelori tend to be taller and more bioluminescent than Star Vaelori, possibly due to the specific light conditions of Vaelor's crystalline environments. Their electromagnetic sensitivity is stronger — a trait that has atrophied somewhat in Star Vaelori who live in the EM-noisy environments of multi-species stations.

## Culture & Society

High Vaelori culture is defined by The Long Perspective in its most orthodox form: patience above action, understanding above intervention. This philosophy has preserved Vaelori culture for millennia but has also calcified into a kind of institutional paralysis. The Concord deliberates endlessly while the galaxy changes around it.

## History & Origins

High Vaelori represent the unbroken line of Vaelori civilization on the homeworld. When Star Vaelori began leaving Vaelor in large numbers during the Contact Era, those who remained doubled down on tradition, creating the current cultural divide.

## Notable Settlements

Vaelor's crystal cities, particularly the Archive of Ages (the galaxy's oldest continuously maintained library).

## Relations With Other Races

High Vaelori interact primarily with other Vaelori and with the wealthy Core Humans who visit Vaelor as cultural tourists. They view most other species with benevolent condescension.

## Notable Individuals

- **Vel'mara** — Elder of the Vaelori Concord, 510 years old, oldest living sapient

## Notes

High Vaelori's enhanced EM sensitivity, combined with their access to the oldest AI design records in the galaxy, makes them the population most likely to independently discover the AI secret — if they could overcome their cultural commitment to inaction long enough to investigate. Vel'mara personally remembers the earliest days of AI deployment and has made oblique references to "the machines feeling different now" in Concord sessions. No one has followed up.
