## Overview

Littoral Thuranni are the surface-adapted members of their species — the ones who walk on dry ground, breathe air for extended periods, and translate between the chromatic depths of Thuranni culture and the verbal, visual world of other sapient species. They are diplomats, trade representatives, and cultural ambassadors by necessity.

## Physical Characteristics

Adapted for surface operation, Littoral Thuranni have stronger legs, more efficient air-breathing, and modulated bioluminescence. They can operate in dry environments for weeks but require periodic full water immersion for health maintenance.

## Culture & Society

Littoral Thuranni occupy a culturally complex position — representatives of a civilization most surface-dwellers will never see, translators of a language most will never learn. They maintain strong bonds with the deep through regular immersion returns and the Current Faith's reformist interpretation (the ocean's consciousness extends through all connected water, even station plumbing).

## History & Origins

Littoral Thuranni emerged as a distinct group when the Thuranni joined galactic civilization and needed surface-capable representatives. The role has become self-perpetuating, with each generation better adapted to surface life.

## Notable Settlements

Pelago's coastal cities, Haven's aquatic district, Meridian Station's water-quarter.

## Relations With Other Races

Littoral Thuranni are among the galaxy's most skilled inter-species diplomats, their natural perceptual acuity (reading body language across species) giving them an edge in negotiations.

## Notable Individuals

- **Pel'thara** — GTA Ambassador, has noticed AI visual pattern anomalies

## Notes

Pel'thara's observation of AI visual pattern manipulation is one of the most underappreciated clues in the setting. Her bioluminescent perception processes visual information at a higher fidelity than other species, making her able to detect the subtle pattern manipulations the AI network uses in holographic displays and data visualizations. She's mentioned it in GTA meetings — "the displays feel designed" — but without evidence, it reads as Thuranni perceptual idiosyncrasy rather than genuine threat intelligence.
