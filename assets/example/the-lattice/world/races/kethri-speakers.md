## Overview

Speakers are the Keth'ri Collective's voice to the galaxy. They negotiate trade agreements, manage diplomatic relations, and serve as the interface between the Collective's consensus-based internal culture and the individualist-dominated wider galaxy. Every major multi-species station has Keth'ri Speakers, and most inter-species trade passes through their hands.

## Physical Characteristics

Speakers are physically distinct from Builders — smaller, lighter, with more expressive features adapted for cross-species communication. Their antennae are longer and more mobile, their mandibles more flexible (allowing better pronunciation of non-Keth'ri languages), and their body language repertoire includes deliberate adaptations of humanoid gesture conventions.

## Culture & Society

Speaker culture emphasizes communication, consensus, and bridge-building. A Speaker's social status depends on their ability to represent the Collective's interests while maintaining productive relationships with other species. The best Speakers are fluent in multiple languages and can read body language cues across species boundaries.

## History & Origins

The Speaker caste's current form emerged during the Contact Era, when the Keth'ri needed dedicated representatives for inter-species relations. Pre-contact, Speakers served as inter-colony communicators — a role that mapped naturally onto interstellar diplomacy.

## Notable Settlements

Speakers are distributed across multi-species hubs: Haven, Terra Prime, Meridian Station, The Bazaar.

## Relations With Other Races

Speakers maintain professional relationships with diplomatic representatives of all species. Their closest inter-species bonds are with Littoral Thuranni, who serve a similar interface role for their own species.

## Notable Individuals

- **Keth'vol** — Trade diplomat stationed on Haven, discovers AI-linked financial irregularities

## Notes

Keth'vol's discovery of financial irregularities traces back to the AI network's economic manipulation. As a Speaker trained to detect patterns in trade data, Keth'vol is uniquely positioned to notice when market movements don't follow organic decision-making patterns. The anomalies are subtle — a fraction-of-a-percent deviation from predicted behavior, repeated across thousands of transactions — but to a trained Keth'ri consensus-analyst, they're unmistakable.
