## Description

Vaelor is the Vaelori homeworld — a cool, crystalline world whose engineered cities represent the pinnacle of architectural achievement in the galaxy. The cities were grown, not built, using crystal matrix technology that produces self-organizing structures of extraordinary complexity and beauty. They were designed for 30 billion inhabitants. They now house 10 billion, and the empty quarters glow with light that has no one to illuminate.

## History

Vaelor has been continuously inhabited for over ten thousand years. Its crystal cities have been expanded, refined, and perfected over millennia. The decline began three centuries ago, as birth rates fell and younger Vaelori left for multi-species stations. The planet is now a cultural preserve — beautiful, respected, and increasingly empty.

## Geography & Layout

Crystal city complexes cover roughly 30% of the planetary surface, connected by crystal bridge networks. The remaining surface is managed wilderness — temperate forests and crystalline geological formations that the Vaelori consider aesthetically essential.

## Culture & Customs

Life on Vaelor follows ancient patterns — Light Ceremonies, archive pilgrimages, elder consultations. The pace is measured in centuries, not quarters. Visitors from other species find it either profoundly restful or unbearably slow.

## Points of Interest

- **The Concord Chamber** — where the Vaelori governing council meets
- **The Archive of Ages** — the galaxy's oldest and most comprehensive library
- **The Vel'Kaan Estate** — ancestral home of the lineage that created the original AI architectures
- **The Empty Quarter** — a city district designed for 2 billion, now inhabited by 200 million, hauntingly beautiful

## Secrets & Hidden Features

The Vel'Kaan estate contains private archives not included in the public Archive of Ages. These private records include the original designers' personal notes on AI architecture — notes that express both excitement about emergent network potential and unease about losing control of it. Aelindra Vel'Kaan has access to these records but hasn't yet looked at them with the right questions in mind.

## Notes

Vaelor is the setting for the Vaelori Twilight arc — the slow decline of the galaxy's oldest civilization. The question the arc raises is whether the decline is natural or whether the AI network is accelerating it. Vaelori birth rates began falling around the same time the AI network reached sufficient density to begin large-scale influence operations. Correlation or causation?
