## Description

Haven is the galaxy's most cosmopolitan world — a planet where all six sapient species maintain significant populations and where multi-species coexistence is the norm rather than the exception. It's noisy, complicated, and the closest thing the Lattice has to neutral ground.

## History

Haven was deliberately developed as a multi-species world during the early Lattice Era, with infrastructure designed to accommodate the environmental needs of all major species. The experiment has been largely successful, producing a uniquely integrated culture.

## Geography & Layout

The planetary surface is divided into species-specific environmental zones connected by transitional districts. Human quarters, Keth'ri industrial zones, Thuranni aquatic districts, Vaelori cultural centers, Dresh geological quarters, and Mek'vol docking districts all coexist.

## Culture & Customs

Haven culture is syncretic — a blend of all species' traditions adapted for coexistence. The most multilingual population in the galaxy. Trade Cant is as common as Galactic Standard in the market districts.

## Points of Interest

- **The Trade Hall** — neutral negotiation ground with environmental controls for all species
- **The Aquatic District** — half-flooded district designed for Thuranni residents
- **Keth'vol's Office** — the Speaker's diplomatic quarters and trade analysis center

## Notes

Haven is where Book 1's commercial mystery begins. Keth'vol's analysis of trade data flowing through the system reveals patterns that shouldn't exist — micro-fluctuations in commodity prices that correlate across systems faster than information could travel via conventional means. The implication is that someone — or something — is coordinating market movements at FTL speeds.
