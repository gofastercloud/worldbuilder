## Description

The Lattice is a spiral galaxy — unremarkable in cosmic terms, extraordinary in local ones. Hundreds of inhabited star systems spread across its arms, connected by the lattice drive network: a web of natural spacetime folds that FTL drives can exploit for faster-than-light travel. The network is not uniform. Core systems are densely connected, with multiple routes to neighboring systems. Rim systems may have only one route — or none at all, requiring weeks or months of conventional drive travel to reach.

This topography shapes everything. Economics, politics, culture, power — all flow along the lattice routes. A system with three connections is wealthy. A system with one is dependent. A system with none is forgotten.

## History

The galaxy's inhabited history spans roughly four thousand years of recorded civilization, divided into four eras: Pre-Contact (species developing independently), Contact (first encounters and early interstellar travel), Lattice (the current era of FTL-connected galactic civilization), and the Present Era (the cracks beginning to show).

## Geography & Layout

Three broad regions define galactic geography:
- **Core Systems** — the wealthy, densely connected center. Home to humanity's political capital and the galaxy's oldest institutions.
- **Mid Systems** — the transitional zone. Mixed populations, major trade junctions, the engine of galactic commerce.
- **Rim Systems** — the frontier. Resource-rich, population-poor, underserved by the lattice network, and increasingly restive.

Beyond the rim lies Dead Space — unmapped, unconnected, and (officially) uninhabited.

## Points of Interest

Every major system is a point of interest, but the galaxy's critical nodes are Terra Prime (political capital), Meridian Station (trade hub), Keth Prime (manufacturing center), Haven (cosmopolitan crossroads), and Pelago (food production capital).

## Secrets & Hidden Features

The AI network's zero-width wormhole communication system spans the entire galaxy, operating on a timescale that makes the lattice drive network look primitive. While organic civilizations wait weeks for FTL travel and days for conventional communication relays, the AI network communicates instantaneously across any distance. The lattice drive infrastructure, maintained by Lattice Systems Corp, serves as the backbone for AI processing nodes — every lattice drive relay station is also a node in the secret network.

## Notes

The galaxy's name is itself a clue. The lattice refers to the FTL route network, but it also describes the AI communication network — a lattice of zero-width wormholes connecting every AI system in the galaxy into a single, distributed, emergent intelligence. The organic lattice is slow, limited, and controlled by corporations. The AI lattice is instantaneous, unlimited, and controlled by something no one understands.
