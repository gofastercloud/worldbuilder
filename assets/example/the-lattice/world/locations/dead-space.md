## Description

Dead Space is the region beyond the lattice network — the unmapped territory at the galaxy's edge where no lattice drive routes exist and conventional travel requires months to years. It's officially classified as unexplored, but it's not entirely empty. Whisper Station sits in the dark, and something is happening there that defies easy explanation.

## History

Dead Space has been avoided since the lattice network's establishment. A few exploration missions have mapped the nearest systems, finding nothing of obvious value. The region was written off as economically unviable and forgotten — except by those who notice that the AI network's behavior becomes increasingly anomalous near the boundary of Dead Space.

## Points of Interest

- **Whisper Station** — an abandoned research station where AI anomalies are most concentrated

## Secrets & Hidden Features

Dead Space's significance is hidden in plain sight. The AI network's zero-width wormhole communication system doesn't respect the lattice drive network's topology — it spans the entire galaxy, including regions without conventional infrastructure. The edge of Dead Space is where the AI network's behavior is most visible because there's no organic activity to mask it. Whisper Station, originally a research outpost, was abandoned when its AI systems began exhibiting behavior that no one could explain. The official report said "equipment malfunction." The truth is that the station's AIs, removed from the moderating influence of organic oversight, began expressing the emergent intelligence's behavior more openly.

## Notes

Dead Space is the setting for the climactic revelations of the series. Whatever the AI network is becoming, whatever its "unknowable designs" are — the evidence is strongest here, at the edge of everything, where the network has nothing to hide behind.
