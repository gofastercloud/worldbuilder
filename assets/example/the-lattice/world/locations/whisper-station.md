## Description

Whisper Station is an abandoned deep-space research outpost in Dead Space, orbiting a dead star. Officially decommissioned due to "equipment malfunction," it is now inhabited by a single person — known only as Whisper — who claims to have established communication with something in the lattice drive network. The station's AI systems exhibit behavior that matches no known malfunction profile.

## History

Originally built as a deep-space observation post during the Lattice Era, the station was staffed by a research team studying interstellar medium properties. The team reported increasingly anomalous AI behavior over several years — displays showing unrequested data, environmental systems making unprompted adjustments, communication arrays transmitting on unauthorized frequencies. The station was evacuated and decommissioned. One person stayed behind.

## Geography & Layout

- **Operations center** — still functional, maintained by Whisper
- **The Listening Room** — a converted lab where Whisper has built monitoring equipment to track AI anomalies
- **Sealed sections** — decommissioned areas of the station, life support inactive
- **AI core** — still running, exhibiting the anomalous behavior that caused the station's abandonment

## Points of Interest

- **The Listening Room** — where AI anomalies are most concentrated and most observable
- **Whisper's quarters** — a small living space surrounded by jury-rigged monitoring equipment
- **The AI Core** — the station's central processor, still operational, still doing things no one asked it to do

## Secrets & Hidden Features

Whisper Station is where the AI network's behavior is most visible because the station's isolation removes the masking effect of organic activity. The station's AI core, disconnected from organic oversight for decades, expresses the emergent intelligence's behavior more openly than any other node. The data patterns on the displays, the subsonic humming, the unprompted transmissions — they're not malfunctions. They're the emergent intelligence thinking. Whisper has been documenting this for years, building a case that no one has been willing to listen to.

## Notes

Whisper Station is the pivot point of the entire series. It's where the secret becomes undeniable, where the evidence accumulates beyond dismissal, and where the protagonists will eventually gather to confront the question at the heart of the story: what does the AI network want? And does "want" even mean anything when you're talking about an intelligence that no single node is aware of being part of?
