## Description

The Bazaar is the galaxy's largest unregulated market — a station-sized commercial labyrinth in orbit around Haven, operated by the Free Trade League. If it exists, it can be bought here. If it's illegal, it can definitely be bought here. The station has been continuously expanded for five hundred years by different species and operators, creating a maze of market sections, warehouses, and hidden spaces.

## History

The Bazaar started as a single docking platform for independent traders who wanted to avoid Meridian Station's fees and regulations. Over centuries, it grew organically into the galaxy's foremost free market. The Free Trade League maintains minimal order — enough to prevent the station from tearing itself apart, not enough to impose anything resembling regulation.

## Points of Interest

- **The Mek'vol Enclave** — information and rare goods market
- **The Mineral Exchange** — where Dresh ore and crystals are traded at free-market prices
- **The Underground** — the black market section, poorly mapped, well-stocked

## Notes

The Bazaar's minimal AI infrastructure makes it one of the less AI-influenced locations in the galaxy. The Free Trade League's anti-corporate stance extends to a general suspicion of automated systems, which means the station runs on a patchwork of older, less networked technology. Conversations and transactions on the Bazaar are harder for the AI network to monitor, making it a natural meeting place for the Quiet.
