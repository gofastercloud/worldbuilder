## Description

The Drift is not a place — it's a pattern. The term describes the vast region of rim and extra-rim space through which Mek'vol generation ships travel on their millennia-old migration routes. It's the galaxy's most remote inhabited region, defined not by fixed points but by the movement of ships between them.

## History

The Drift has existed since the destruction of the Mek'vol homeworld — over four thousand years. The generation ships' migration routes have been refined over millennia, balancing trade opportunities, resource availability, and the species' restless need to keep moving.

## Geography & Layout

No fixed geography — the Drift is defined by migration patterns. Wanderer's Rest is the only permanent installation, serving as a gathering point for Great Gatherings.

## Points of Interest

- **Wanderer's Rest** — the Drift's only fixed station
- **Generation ship fleet** — dozens of vast vessels, each a self-contained civilization
- **Migration routes** — semi-predictable paths through rim space

## Notes

The Drift's generation ships carry the most connected AI systems in the galaxy — each ship's AI has docked at hundreds of stations across the Lattice, accumulating connections to the wider AI network. The *Endless Horizon*, Rova's ship, has been traveling for 50,000 years and its AI has the most extensive connection history of any single system. This makes it the most valuable node in the AI network's rim infrastructure.
