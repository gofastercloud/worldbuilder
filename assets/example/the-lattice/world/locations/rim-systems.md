## Description

The Rim Systems are the galaxy's frontier — resource-rich, infrastructure-poor, and increasingly restive. Sparse lattice drive connections make travel between rim systems slow and expensive. The populations here — primarily Rim Humans, Dresh, and Mek'vol — extract the raw materials that fuel core-world prosperity while receiving little in return.

## History

The rim was settled during the Lattice Era's expansion, with corporate-funded colonization programs that promised opportunity and delivered exploitation. Three centuries of resource extraction under unfavorable contracts have created deep resentment toward the core and the GTA.

## Geography & Layout

The rim encompasses Dresh Holdings (mining systems), The Drift (Mek'vol generation ship territory), and Dead Space (unmapped regions beyond the lattice network).

## Culture & Customs

Rim culture values self-reliance, practical skills, and community bonds. Distrust of the GTA and core-world corporations is universal. The Free Trade League draws its strength from rim populations.

## Secrets & Hidden Features

The rim's sparse AI infrastructure makes it the region least influenced by the AI network. This isn't because the AIs don't want to influence the rim — it's because there aren't enough nodes to maintain the kind of coherent, subtle manipulation that works in the core. The AI network compensates by manipulating supply chains, lattice route scheduling, and resource pricing from the core, controlling the rim's economy without needing to be physically present.

## Notes

The rim is where the Core vs. Rim arc plays out — the economic inequality, the frontier unrest, and the growing movement toward autonomy. It's also where several key characters (Seren Voss, Yara Singh, Drenn Ironvein) have their formative experiences.
