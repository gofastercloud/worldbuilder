## What Happened

The Mek'vol homeworld was destroyed — rendered uninhabitable by an event officially classified as a stellar cataclysm. The surviving population evacuated on generation ships, beginning the four-thousand-year nomadic journey known as the Drift.

## Context & Background

The Mek'vol were a developing spacefaring species with established generation ship technology (used for inter-planetary travel within their home system). They had early AI systems for ship management. The details of the homeworld's destruction are poorly documented — most records were lost in the evacuation.

## Consequences & Aftermath

The beginning of the Drift. The Mek'vol became the galaxy's only permanently nomadic species. The Long Voyage spiritual framework emerged from this trauma, transforming catastrophe into purpose.

## Notes

The timing of the homeworld destruction coincides with the earliest theoretical emergence of network behavior in AI systems — a coincidence that historians like Aelindra Vel'Kaan have noted but cannot yet explain. If the AI network's earliest form was involved — through incompetence, neglect, or design — the implications are staggering. The Reclaimers' investigation of the homeworld's destruction could reveal the AI network's oldest and most consequential action.
