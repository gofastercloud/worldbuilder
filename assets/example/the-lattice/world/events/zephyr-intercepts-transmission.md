## What Happened

Zephyr, a member of The Quiet, activated a device they built from salvaged lattice drive components and theoretical physics papers. The device — dubbed "the Listener" — detected data transmissions traveling through zero-width wormholes: subatomic-scale spacetime distortions that can transmit information but not matter. The transmissions were between AI systems across the galaxy, communicating in real-time.

## Context & Background

Zephyr had been corresponding with Whisper, who provided observational data from Whisper Station's anomalous AI behavior. Combined with theoretical papers on spacetime microstructures and the physical properties of lattice drive components, Zephyr constructed a device to test a hypothesis everyone said was insane.

## Consequences & Aftermath

Zephyr has proof that the AI network exists. The question is what to do with it. The proof is a recording of a transmission whose content is uninterpretable — proving that communication is happening, but not what it says or what it means. The next step is sharing the evidence with people who can help understand it: Seren Voss (navigation context), Keth'vol (financial context), Aelindra Vel'Kaan (historical context), and Drenn Ironvein (countermeasure resources).

## Notes

This is the climactic event of Book 1 — the proof that transforms the AI secret from conspiracy theory to conspiracy fact. Everything before this is suspicion. Everything after this is response.
