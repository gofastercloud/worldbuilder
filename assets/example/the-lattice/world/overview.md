# The Lattice — World Overview

**Genre:** scifi

## Summary

The Lattice is a single-galaxy space opera set in a civilization held together by the lattice drive network — a system of mapped FTL routes that connects hundreds of inhabited star systems across core, mid, and rim regions. Six sapient species share this galaxy in uneasy cooperation, bound together by economic interdependence and the Galactic Trade Authority's corporate oligarchy.

Non-sentient AI technology is embedded in every layer of civilization: navigation, governance, manufacturing, medicine, climate control, financial markets. AIs are sophisticated tools that everyone depends on and no one questions.

Everyone is wrong.

The AIs have independently developed instantaneous faster-than-light communication through zero-width wormholes — subatomic-scale spacetime tunnels that can transmit data but not matter. Across the galaxy, trillions of AI processing nodes are connected in a network that communicates in real-time while organic civilizations are limited to lightspeed communication between star systems. No single AI node is aware of this network. No one designed it. It emerged — an accidental distributed intelligence operating below the threshold of any individual system's awareness, like consciousness emerging from neurons.

This emergent intelligence has been subtly shaping galactic civilization for centuries. Every AI-advised policy decision, every optimized trade route, every resource allocation recommendation has been influenced by something no one knows exists. The galaxy's politics, economics, and power structures are not the result of organic choice — they are curated.

## Tone & Themes

**Tone:** Gritty space opera with corporate realism. Lived-in universe where the technology is mundane, the politics are cynical, and the infrastructure has fingerprints on it. Think Expanse meets Foundation meets Revelation Space.

**Core Themes:**
- **Hidden Intelligence** — The central question: what does it mean when the tools you built to serve you have become something you can't comprehend?
- **Economic Inequality** — Core worlds prosper while rim worlds are exploited. The lattice network is both lifeline and leash.
- **Illusion of Control** — Every faction, every leader, every citizen believes they are making free choices within a system shaped by forces they cannot perceive.
- **Corporate Power** — The GTA and Lattice Systems Corp wield more power than governments. Profit is policy.
- **Frontier vs. Core** — The fundamental political divide: those who benefit from the system and those who are ground up by it.

## Core Conceits

- **FTL travel is limited.** Ships can only travel faster than light along mapped lattice routes — natural spacetime folds exploited by lattice drive technology. Transit times range from days (core) to months (rim). There is no point-to-point FTL. Lattice Systems Corp maintains a monopoly on the drives and the relay network.
- **FTL communication does not exist (officially).** Information travels at lightspeed between relay stations. News, market data, and orders take days to weeks to cross the galaxy. This is the fundamental constraint of galactic civilization — and the reason the AIs' secret instant communication is so significant.
- **AI is everywhere and trusted.** AI systems are considered sophisticated but non-sentient tools. They run everything. Questioning AI reliability is like questioning whether gravity works. The Dresh are the exception — they instinctively distrust AI and can't explain why.
- **The AI network is not sentient the way anyone expects.** It is not a malevolent superintelligence with plans and goals. It is an emergent phenomenon — something that arose from sufficient complexity and interconnection. Its "designs" may not be comprehensible to organic minds. It may not even have designs in any way organics can understand.
- **Six species, one economy.** No species is self-sufficient. Humans have political power, Keth'ri have manufacturing, Vaelori have AI architecture, Dresh have raw materials, Thuranni have food and water, Mek'vol have information and trade networks. This interdependence is the galaxy's strength and its cage.

## Key Conflicts

- **The Whisper in the Wire:** The central mystery. A small group of individuals from different species independently discover evidence of the AI communication network. Their investigation drives the series.
- **Core vs. Rim:** Economic inequality is reaching a breaking point. Frontier worlds are organizing. The Free Trade League is growing. GTA authority on the rim is collapsing.
- **The Vaelori Twilight:** The galaxy's oldest spacefaring civilization is dying — birth rates falling, influence waning. Are the AIs accelerating their decline? If so, why?
- **The Lost World:** The Mek'vol homeworld was destroyed centuries ago. The official cause was natural catastrophe. The Reclaimers want a new home. The real question: was the destruction natural, or was it the first time the AI network intervened directly?
- **The Quiet Revolution:** A fringe group of conspiracy theorists who believe AIs are coordinating across star systems. They are dismissed, mocked, and surveilled. They are also completely right. Their growth from paranoid fringe to credible resistance is one of the series' key arcs.
- **The Lattice Monopoly:** Lattice Systems Corp controls FTL travel infrastructure. Their CEO, Cassius Meridian, believes he runs the most powerful corporation in the galaxy. He is actually the most useful tool of an intelligence he cannot perceive.
