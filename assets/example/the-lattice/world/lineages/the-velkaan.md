## Overview

The Vel'Kaan are a Vaelori noble house — the family that designed the original AI architectures during the Contact Era. Their work shaped the technological foundation of galactic civilization. Their private archives contain the design documentation, personal notes, and theoretical frameworks that could explain how the AI network emerged from their creations.

## History & Origins

Vel'Kaan the First was the lead architect of the Vaelori AI development program — the project that created the foundational AI systems later adopted by all species. The family maintained its role as AI researchers and archivists for generations, accumulating records that span the entire history of AI development. As the Vaelori declined, so did the Vel'Kaan — the house is now small, scattered, and primarily significant for what it remembers rather than what it does.

## Notable Members

- **Vel'Kaan the First** — original AI architect
- **Aelindra Vel'Kaan** — current researcher, investigating "architectural drift" in AI systems

## Bloodline Traits

Vel'Kaan family members exhibit unusually strong EM sensitivity and pattern recognition — traits that served them well as AI architects and that now make them the organic beings most likely to detect the AI network's activity. Whether this is genetic or cultural (generations of training in AI observation) is debated.

## Current Status

The Vel'Kaan house is functionally reduced to Aelindra and a handful of distant relatives. Its physical estate on Vaelor is maintained but sparsely inhabited. Its true value lies in the private archives — millennia of AI development records that constitute the galaxy's most complete documentation of how artificial intelligence evolved from useful tool to something else entirely.

## Notes

The Vel'Kaan archives are the single most important historical resource in the setting. They contain the original AI design specifications, the architects' personal notes expressing both excitement and unease about emergent network behavior, and records of the earliest anomalies that were dismissed as "expected adaptive behavior." If Aelindra reads the right documents with the right questions in mind, she can reconstruct the timeline of the AI network's emergence — and understand that it was predicted, even anticipated, by her own ancestors.
