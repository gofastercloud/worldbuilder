## Description

The Free Trade League is a loose coalition of independent traders, frontier operators, and anti-corporate activists who oppose the GTA's regulatory authority and Lattice Systems Corp's monopoly on FTL travel. They're the closest thing the galaxy has to an organized opposition movement.

## History & Founding

The League coalesced during the middle Lattice Era from disparate frontier trading networks that found common cause in opposing GTA taxation and LSC's lattice drive monopoly. It has no single founder — it emerged from collective frustration the way the AI network emerged from collective computation.

## Structure & Hierarchy

Deliberately decentralized — no single leader, no fixed hierarchy. Decisions are made by consensus among local chapters, which means the League is simultaneously everywhere and nowhere. This decentralization makes it resilient but disorganized.

## Beliefs & Values

Economic freedom, rim independence, anti-monopoly. The League believes the galaxy's wealth should be distributed more equitably and that no single corporation should control FTL infrastructure.

## Territory & Influence

The Bazaar (unofficial headquarters), rim trading posts, independent stations. Influence strongest in rim systems where GTA authority is weakest.

## Notes

The League's decentralized, low-tech operational style inadvertently provides some protection from AI network influence. Their communications use older, less networked technology. Their decision-making is face-to-face. Their distrust of corporate infrastructure extends to a general suspicion of sophisticated AI systems. None of this is deliberate anti-AI strategy, but it amounts to the same thing.
