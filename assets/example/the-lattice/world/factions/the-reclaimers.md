## Description

The Reclaimers are a growing faction within Mek'vol society that seeks a new homeworld for the species. This directly challenges The Long Voyage — the spiritual framework that has defined Mek'vol identity since the destruction of their original homeworld. The movement is culturally radical, politically divisive, and increasingly popular among younger Mek'vol.

## History & Founding

The Reclaimer movement has existed in some form since the early Drift, but it gained political traction during the Present Era as younger Mek'vol began questioning whether four thousand years of nomadism was a choice or a habit. The movement has a militant wing that advocates for claiming a suitable world by force if necessary.

## Structure & Hierarchy

Loosely organized around charismatic ship-captains and community leaders. No single leader, but influential voices (including Rova, who is sympathetic though not fully committed) shape the movement's direction.

## Beliefs & Values

Settlement is survival. The Drift is not destiny but exile. The Mek'vol deserve what every other species has — a homeworld.

## Territory & Influence

Based primarily on Wanderer's Rest and sympathetic generation ships. Influence growing among younger Mek'vol across the fleet.

## Notes

The Reclaimers' interest in the homeworld's destruction opens a direct path to the AI secret. If they investigate what destroyed the Mek'vol homeworld — really investigate, with modern analytical tools — they may discover that early AI systems were involved. This thread connects the Mek'vol storyline to the overarching AI narrative.
