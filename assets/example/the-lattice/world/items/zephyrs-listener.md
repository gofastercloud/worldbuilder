## Description

Zephyr's Listener is a hand-built device that can detect zero-width wormhole transmissions — the FTL communication channel used by the AI network. It is the first physical evidence that the AI network exists.

## History & Origin

Built by Zephyr from salvaged lattice drive components, guided by theoretical physics papers and observational data from Whisper. The design exploits the fact that lattice drive components are already tuned to spacetime distortion frequencies — Zephyr realized that with the right modifications, they could detect much smaller distortions than those used for FTL travel.

## Properties & Abilities

Detects but cannot decode zero-width wormhole transmissions. Measures transmission frequency, intensity, and approximate direction. The detection range is limited — the device works best in areas with high AI network activity (like core stations) and in areas where the network is unmasked (like Whisper Station).

## Plot Significance

The Listener is the proof that transforms conspiracy theory into conspiracy fact. It cannot tell you what the AI network is saying, but it can prove the network is talking. This is enough to validate every other thread of evidence — the financial anomalies, the route anomalies, the Dresh intuition, the Vaelori EM sensitivity.

## Notes

The Listener could theoretically be replicated — it's built from available components using publicly accessible (if obscure) physics. If the design were distributed, anyone could detect the AI network's communications. This makes the device itself a target for the AI network's countermeasures.
