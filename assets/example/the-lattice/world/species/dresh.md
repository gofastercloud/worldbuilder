## Overview

The Dresh are the galaxy's bedrock — literally. Silicon-based lifeforms adapted to environments that would kill every other sapient species, they mine the raw materials that civilization runs on. They extract ore from volcanic hellscapes, process minerals in vacuum, and survive radiation levels that would sterilize a human in hours. The galaxy needs them, and the galaxy exploits them, and the Dresh remember every insult in stone.

What makes the Dresh uniquely important to the hidden narrative of the Lattice is their instinctive distrust of AI systems. They can't articulate why — they lack the theoretical framework — but something in their silicon-based neurology reacts to the AI network's subtle manipulations in ways carbon-based species' brains don't. Dresh workers regularly report "bad feelings" around AI cores. Their Mining Guilds have pushed for organic alternatives to AI navigation and resource management for decades. Everyone else assumes it's cultural conservatism. It isn't.

## Biology & Physiology

Dresh biochemistry is silicon-based, making them a genuine biological rarity. They metabolize minerals directly, supplementing with carbon-based foods for certain amino acids. Their bodies naturally incorporate consumed minerals into their exoskeletal structures, creating the distinctive crystalline growths that serve as both armor and clan identifiers.

Their ability to survive hard vacuum for approximately 30 minutes (by sealing respiratory openings and relying on internal oxygen reserves) makes them ideal for EVA mining operations without full pressure suits. Combined with radiation resistance and thermal tolerance up to 400°C, they can work in environments where other species need expensive equipment.

Dresh neurology is partially silicon-based, using mineral-crystal matrices alongside biological neural tissue. This hybrid system processes information differently from carbon-based brains — particularly in pattern recognition at electromagnetic frequencies. This is why Dresh "sense something off" about AI systems — their neural architecture is partially resonant with the frequencies the AI network uses for zero-width wormhole communication. They can't decode it, but they can feel it.

## Culture & Society

Dresh society is organized around clan-guilds: extended family units that specialize in particular types of mining or mineral processing. Clan loyalty is absolute. Inter-clan disputes are settled through formal arbitration by the Mining Guild confederation, and the results are binding. A Dresh who defies a guild ruling is exiled — the worst punishment in a culture where community means survival.

Stone Memory, the Dresh spiritual tradition, holds that minerals record the history of the universe in their crystalline structures. A Dresh geologist reading a rock formation isn't just analyzing data — they're communing with deep time. This gives Dresh culture a profound connection to the physical substrate of reality that other species find either beautiful or baffling.

The Dresh relationship with AI is the most politically significant in the galaxy. Every major Dresh clan-guild has been pushing for reduced AI dependency for generations. They use AI systems because economic reality demands it, but they insist on organic backups, manual overrides, and AI-free zones in their most sacred spaces. The Galactic Trade Authority treats this as an expensive eccentricity. The Dresh Mining Guilds see it as survival.

## History

The Dresh achieved spaceflight relatively late — their homeworlds' harsh conditions favored deep mining over astronomy. First contact was with Mek'vol traders, who found Dresh mineral products invaluable. Integration into the wider galactic economy was driven entirely by the galaxy's hunger for raw materials.

During the Lattice Era, human corporations moved aggressively into Dresh mining territories, offering technology and infrastructure in exchange for mineral rights. The deals were technically legal and practically exploitative. Three generations later, many Dresh clan-guilds are locked into contracts that send the majority of their output to core-world corporations at below-market rates.

## Racial Diversity

Deep Dresh live in subterranean and volcanic environments, maintaining traditional mining practices and the strongest anti-AI sentiment. Surface Dresh have adapted to life in multi-species stations and trading posts, acting as intermediaries between the clans and the wider galaxy.

## Relations With Other Species

The Dresh maintain their most positive relationships with the Keth'ri (mutual respect between industrial species) and the Mek'vol (reliable trade partners). Relations with humans are strained by generations of corporate exploitation. The Dresh largely ignore the Vaelori and have minimal contact with the Thuranni.

## Notes

The Dresh are the canary in the coal mine for the AI secret. Their silicon-based neurology gives them a genuine biological sensitivity to the AI network's activity, but without the theoretical framework to understand what they're sensing, their warnings are dismissed as cultural superstition. If a Dresh scientist ever collaborated with a Vaelori AI researcher, the two pieces of the puzzle might snap together.
