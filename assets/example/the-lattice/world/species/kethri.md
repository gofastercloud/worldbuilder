## Overview

The Keth'ri are the galaxy's builders. If it has a hull, a structural beam, or a functioning life-support system, odds are good that Keth'ri hands built it — all four of them. Their cultural consensus model, often mischaracterized as a "hive mind" by ignorant humans, is actually a deeply democratic process where decisions emerge from extensive collective deliberation. No Keth'ri speaks for the collective without the collective's agreement, and no Keth'ri acts alone if they can act together.

This consensus-driven culture makes them exceptional engineers and manufacturers but poor politicians in the individualist-dominated Galactic Trade Authority. They consistently get outmaneuvered in trade negotiations by species that can make snap decisions.

## Biology & Physiology

Keth'ri are insectoid bipeds with four arms — a heavy upper pair for strength work and a dexterous lower pair for fine manipulation. Their chitinous exoskeleton provides natural armor and vacuum resistance for brief periods (15-20 minutes), making them ideal shipyard workers. Compound eyes give them near-360-degree vision with excellent motion detection but mediocre color perception.

Their short lifespans (40-60 years) are offset by rapid maturation — a Keth'ri reaches physical adulthood in 5 years and full cognitive development by 8. This means they cycle through generations roughly three times faster than humans, which gives their civilization an unusual dynamism despite its consensus-based decision-making.

Keth'ri communicate natively through a combination of mandible clicks, antennae vibrations, and subsonic rumbles. They can learn to produce Galactic Standard speech, but it always sounds slightly mechanical — the mandibles aren't built for the soft consonants of human languages.

## Culture & Society

Keth'ri society is organized around work-collectives: groups of 50-500 individuals who live, work, and deliberate together. Major decisions require consensus within a collective, and inter-collective decisions require consensus between representative Speakers. This process can take days or weeks for significant matters, which humans find maddeningly slow.

The Pattern — the closest thing the Keth'ri have to a religion — is the belief that all systems tend toward optimal arrangement given sufficient iteration. It's part engineering philosophy, part metaphysics. A Keth'ri Builder who creates an elegant solution to a structural problem is having a religious experience.

Keth'ri don't have families in the human sense. Eggs are laid communally, and young are raised by the collective. Individual parentage is known but not emphasized. This communal approach to child-rearing is another reason humans mischaracterize them as a "hive mind."

## History

The Keth'ri were the second species to achieve spaceflight, after the Vaelori. Their early space program was characteristically methodical — no heroic solo pilots, just collectives of engineers solving problems together until the problems were solved. First contact with humans was rocky (two brief wars), but the Keth'ri recognized the economic logic of cooperation faster than humans did.

During the Lattice Era, the Keth'ri Collective became the galaxy's dominant manufacturer. Their shipyards around Keth Prime produce 60% of all new spacecraft. This economic position gives them significant leverage, but their consensus-based politics prevent them from using it as aggressively as a human corporation would.

## Racial Diversity

Two major castes (which function more like cultural roles than biological subspecies): Builders, who focus on engineering and construction, and Speakers, who handle diplomacy and trade. The distinction is partly physical (Builders are larger) and partly cultural (Speakers receive additional training in non-Keth'ri communication).

## Relations With Other Species

The Keth'ri maintain the galaxy's strongest manufacturing partnerships. They build ships for everyone, maintain stations for the GTA, and supply critical components to Lattice Systems Corp. This makes them indispensable — and makes their occasional threats to slow production a powerful negotiating tool.

- **Overseer Kryx** — Builder, manages Keth Prime shipyards
- **Thek'vol** — Builder, Lattice Systems Corp R&D, Keth'vol's sibling

## Notes

The Keth'ri short lifespan creates an interesting dynamic with the AI secret. No individual Keth'ri lives long enough to notice the multi-generational patterns of AI influence, but their collective records span thousands of years. If anyone were to data-mine Keth'ri industrial records for anomalies, they might find evidence of the AI network's interference — but no one has thought to look.
