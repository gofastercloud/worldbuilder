## Overview

Humans are the most numerous and politically dominant species in the Lattice. They didn't get here by being the smartest, the strongest, or the most ancient — the Vaelori had spaceflight while humans were still arguing about heliocentrism. Humans got here by being relentless, adaptable, and willing to exploit every advantage, including other species.

The human story in the Lattice is one of corporate ascendancy. Where other species organized around collective identity (Keth'ri), ancient tradition (Vaelori), or clan loyalty (Dresh), humans organized around profit. The Galactic Trade Authority is technically multi-species, but its power structures, its culture, and its priorities are overwhelmingly human.

## Biology & Physiology

Baseline human biology remains largely unchanged from ancient Terra. What has changed is the envelope of augmentation layered on top. Core humans routinely receive neural interfaces, metabolic regulators, bone-density enhancements, and immune system boosters. These augmentations are so normalized in core society that an unaugmented human is viewed with the same mild pity as someone who can't read.

Rim humans, lacking access to expensive augmentation, have instead adapted through generations of natural selection on harsh worlds. Higher radiation tolerance, denser bone structure, more efficient oxygen processing. Some frontier populations have diverged enough that geneticists debate subspecies classification — a politically toxic topic.

Human lifespan has been extended to 120-150 years through medical technology, though quality of life in the final decades varies enormously between core and rim.

## Culture & Society

Human culture in the Lattice is defined by a single axis: proximity to the core. Core humans live in a post-scarcity illusion — AIs manage infrastructure, corporations provide employment, and consumer culture fills the gaps. Status is measured in corporate rank, augmentation quality, and access to the right stations.

Rim humans inhabit a different universe. Resources are scarce, corporate presence is extractive rather than supportive, and survival depends on community bonds that core humans would find uncomfortably intimate. Rim culture values self-reliance, practical skills, and a deep suspicion of anything that arrives on a corporate ship.

## History

Humans achieved independent spaceflight in the Pre-Contact era and were the third species to do so (after the Vaelori and Keth'ri). First contact with the Vaelori was peaceful — the Vaelori were already in decline and saw humans as potential inheritors rather than threats. First contact with the Keth'ri was less smooth, resulting in two brief wars before economic interdependence made peace more profitable than conflict.

The founding of the Galactic Trade Authority in the early Lattice Era cemented human political dominance. The lattice drive network — maintained by the human-founded Lattice Systems Corp — gave humans control over the single most critical infrastructure in the galaxy.

## Racial Diversity

Two broad categories have emerged: Core Humans, the augmented elite of the wealthy inner systems, and Rim Humans, the hardscrabble frontier populations. The divide is as much cultural and economic as it is biological, but generations of different selection pressures are making it increasingly physical as well.

## Relations With Other Species

Humans maintain functional relationships with all other species, driven primarily by economic necessity. They depend on Keth'ri manufacturing, Vaelori AI architecture, Dresh raw materials, Thuranni food production, and Mek'vol trade networks. This mutual dependence is the glue holding the Lattice together — and the source of most of its tensions.

## Notes

Humans are the "default" species in the Lattice — the baseline against which other species are measured. This is itself a political statement that other species resent. The Galactic Standard language, the standard calendar, the credit system — all are human-derived or human-controlled.
